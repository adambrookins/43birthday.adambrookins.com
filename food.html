Off the grill

The Traeger does the heavy lifting. Deep smoke, big flavor, zero rush.

Carne Asada Street Tacos

Skirt steak in a citrus-soy-garlic marinade, seared screaming hot, chopped and piled on doubled corn tortillas with onion, cilantro, and a charred-tomatillo salsa.

Crowd favorite
Recipe

You need

  • 3 lb skirt or flank steak
  • ½ cup soy sauce, juice of 3 limes + 1 orange
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, minced; big handful cilantro
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp black pepper
  • Corn tortillas, white onion, cilantro, salsa verde

Make it

  1. Marinate steak 4–12 hrs in everything but the tortillas.
  2. Grill screaming hot, 3–4 min per side to medium-rare.
  3. Rest 10 min, dice small.
  4. Serve on doubled, griddle-warmed tortillas with onion, cilantro, and salsa.

Elote Bar

Grilled sweet corn rolled in crema and cotija, dusted with Tajín and lime. Serve on the cob or as esquites in cups so pool hands stay clean-ish.

Veggie friendly
Recipe

You need

  • 8–10 ears sweet corn, husked
  • ½ cup Mexican crema (or mayo/sour cream mix)
  • 1 cup crumbled cotija
  • Tajín, chili powder, 2 limes in wedges
  • Butter, salt

Make it

  1. Grill corn over direct heat ~10 min, turning, until charred in spots.
  2. Butter and salt while hot.
  3. Set out crema, cotija, Tajín, and lime wedges — guests build their own.
  4. For esquites: cut kernels off, toss with the same fixings, serve in cups.

Bacon-Wrapped Jalapeño Poppers

Already a proven hit on this Traeger. Cream cheese and chorizo-stuffed jalapeños, wrapped in bacon, smoked till the bacon crisps. Make double — they vanish.

Traeger classic
Recipe

You need

  • 12 jalapeños, halved lengthwise, seeded
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ lb Mexican chorizo, cooked and cooled
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar
  • 12 slices bacon, halved

Make it

  1. Mix cream cheese, chorizo, and cheddar; fill each jalapeño half.
  2. Wrap each with a half-slice of bacon, seam down.
  3. Smoke at 275°F for ~90 min until bacon is crisp (or grill indirect ~45 min).
  4. Rest 5 min — molten centers are no joke.

Al Pastor Chicken Thighs

Boneless thighs in an achiote-pineapple-guajillo marinade, grilled with pineapple rings alongside. Smoky, sweet, a little heat — taco-able or straight off the platter.

Marinade overnight
Recipe

You need

  • 4 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp achiote paste
  • 3 dried guajillo chiles, soaked and blended (or 2 tbsp chile powder)
  • 1 cup pineapple juice + ¼ cup white vinegar
  • 4 garlic cloves, 1 tsp oregano, 1 tsp cumin, salt
  • 1 fresh pineapple, in rings

Make it

  1. Blend marinade ingredients; coat thighs and rest overnight.
  2. Grill over medium-high ~5 min per side to 165°F internal.
  3. Grill pineapple rings alongside until caramelized.
  4. Chop both together, pile on a platter with cilantro and lime.
Flat-top magic

On the Blackstone

Fast, loud, and best eaten standing dripping wet in a towel.

Smash Burger Tacos

A smash burger ball pressed flat onto a flour tortilla, griddled together, topped with oaxaca cheese, shredded lettuce, and taco-shop sauce. The viral one — and it deserves the hype.

Memorable
Recipe

You need

  • 2 lb 80/20 ground beef in 2.5-oz balls
  • Flour tortillas (taco size)
  • Oaxaca or American cheese
  • Shredded lettuce, diced onion, pickles
  • Sauce: ½ cup mayo + 2 tbsp ketchup + 1 tbsp mustard + splash pickle juice

Make it

  1. Blackstone on high. Press a beef ball flat onto each tortilla.
  2. Place beef-side down, smash hard with a spatula, season, ~2 min until crusty.
  3. Flip, top with cheese, 1 more minute.
  4. Dress with lettuce, onion, pickles, and sauce; fold and go.

Camarones a la Diabla Skewers

Shrimp seared on the flat top in a chipotle-garlic butter that gets spooned over at the end. Two minutes a side. Serve with grilled limes and torn tortillas for mopping.

Fast & fiery
Recipe

You need

  • 2 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 3 chipotles in adobo, minced + 2 tbsp of the sauce
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice of 2 limes, salt
  • Skewers (soaked if wooden)

Make it

  1. Skewer shrimp; season with salt.
  2. Sear on the hot flat top ~2 min per side until just pink.
  3. Melt butter with garlic, chipotle, and adobo on the griddle; toss or spoon over.
  4. Finish with lime. Serve with warm tortillas for mopping.

Quesabirria-Style Quesadillas

Tortillas dipped in consomé, griddled with melty cheese and shredded beef until the edges frizzle and crisp. Cut into wedges, serve with the consomé for dunking.

Dip everything
Recipe

You need

  • 3 lb chuck roast (or use store-bought birria + consomé)
  • 4 dried guajillos + 2 anchos, soaked
  • 1 onion, 5 garlic cloves, 1 tbsp vinegar, 2 tsp oregano, 2 tsp cumin, 2 bay leaves
  • Corn tortillas, 1 lb oaxaca or mozzarella

Make it

  1. Blend chiles, onion, garlic, spices with 2 cups stock; braise beef in it 3 hrs until shreddable (do this the day before).
  2. Skim fat from the consomé — that's your tortilla dip.
  3. Dip tortillas in the fatty consomé, griddle, top with cheese and beef, fold.
  4. Crisp both sides; serve wedges with hot consomé for dunking.

Griddled Pineapple Sundaes

Pineapple rings caramelized in brown sugar butter on the flat top, over vanilla ice cream with Tajín and honey. Dessert, handled — no oven required.

Dessert
Recipe

You need

  • 1 fresh pineapple, cored, in rings
  • 4 tbsp butter + ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream
  • Tajín and honey to finish

Make it

  1. Melt butter with brown sugar and cinnamon on medium griddle.
  2. Add pineapple rings; caramelize 2–3 min per side.
  3. Serve hot over scoops of vanilla.
  4. Drizzle honey, light dust of Tajín. Trust it.
Smoke in a glass

The mezcal bar

Deep, smoky, sipping-weather drinks. One bottle of decent espadín covers all of these.

Oaxaca Old Fashioned

1.5 oz reposado + 0.5 oz mezcal, barspoon of agave, 2 dashes Angostura, big cube, orange peel flamed over the top. The deepest drink on the menu.

Slow sipper
Recipe

You need

  • 1.5 oz reposado tequila
  • 0.5 oz mezcal
  • 1 barspoon agave nectar
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel, big ice cube

Make it

  1. Stir everything with ice ~20 seconds.
  2. Strain over a big cube in a rocks glass.
  3. Express the orange peel over the top (flame it if you're feeling theatrical) and drop it in.

Mezcal Negroni

Equal parts mezcal, Campari, and sweet vermouth over ice with an orange slice. Bitter, smoky, dangerously easy to batch in a pitcher before guests arrive.

Batchable
Recipe

You need

  • 1 oz mezcal
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • Orange slice

Make it

  1. Build in a rocks glass over ice; stir 10 seconds.
  2. Garnish with the orange slice.
  3. Pitcher batch: equal parts of each ×8, stir with ice just before pouring.

Smoky Paloma

2 oz mezcal, squeeze of lime, Squirt or Jarritos grapefruit over ice, Tajín rim. The pool drink — tall, cold, and refillable all afternoon.

Poolside
Recipe

You need

  • 2 oz mezcal
  • ½ oz fresh lime juice
  • Squirt or Jarritos grapefruit, chilled
  • Tajín + salt for the rim, lime wedge

Make it

  1. Rim a tall glass with lime and Tajín-salt; fill with ice.
  2. Add mezcal and lime juice.
  3. Top with grapefruit soda, stir once, wedge on the rim.

Mezcal Carajillo

Licor 43 and a shot of espresso, shaken over ice, floated with 0.5 oz mezcal. Sweet, dark, smoky — the late-afternoon pick-me-up before round two of cannonballs.

After dinner
Recipe

You need

  • 1.5 oz Licor 43
  • 1 shot (1.5 oz) fresh espresso, slightly cooled
  • 0.5 oz mezcal

Make it

  1. Shake Licor 43 and espresso hard with ice ~15 seconds.
  2. Strain over fresh ice.
  3. Float the mezcal on top; sip through the foam.

Red Glam

A mezcal mule variation. Smoky joven mezcal meets muddled raspberry, fresh lime, and spicy ginger beer. Long, sharp, and balanced.

Copper mug
Recipe

You need

  • 2 oz (60 ml) Montelobos or other joven mezcal
  • 6 fresh raspberries
  • ¾ oz (20 ml) fresh lime juice
  • ⅓ oz (10 ml) simple syrup
  • 3.5 oz (100 ml) spicy ginger beer, chilled (Thomas Henry; Fever-Tree is the closest US sub)
  • Lime wheel + raspberries for garnish

Make it

  1. Muddle the raspberries in a shaker until fully crushed.
  2. Add mezcal, lime, simple syrup, and ice. Shake hard 12 seconds.
  3. Double strain through a fine mesh into a copper mug or highball over fresh ice (catches the seeds).
  4. Top with ginger beer, stir once to lift. Garnish with the lime wheel and raspberries.

Notes

  • No fresh raspberries? Use 15 ml raspberry syrup or a barspoon of preserves and skip the simple.
  • Drier version: cut the syrup — the ginger beer carries enough sweetness.
The house pour

The Kirkland margarita

No shame, all flavor. This is the workhorse pitcher of the party.

Pitcher recipe — serves ~8

Costco run: one bottle of Kirkland Silver, limes, and you're most of the way there.
  • 2 cups Kirkland Signature Silver Tequila
  • 1 cup fresh-squeezed lime juice (10–12 limes — fresh matters, this is the whole trick)
  • ¾ cup orange liqueur (Kirkland's triple sec is fine, Cointreau if fancy)
  • ½ cup agave nectar, adjusted to taste
  • 1 big pinch of salt, straight into the pitcher
  • Rim half salt, half Tajín — do a tray of glasses ahead of time
Stir with plenty of ice, taste, and add a splash of water if it bites. Float 0.5 oz of mezcal on top of any glass to upgrade it to the "birthday boy version."
